# Public Works Innovation Challenge — Overview & Submission Guide

Last updated: 2025-12-17

## What It Is
The Innovation Challenge is an opportunity for Public Works employees to explore and showcase innovative ideas that enhance services, address resident needs, and improve workforce quality of life. Modeled after a March Madness-style bracket, teams across divisions propose innovations and compete for a chance to become the Public Works Innovation Champions.

## Why We’re Doing This
- Innovation is a core value and supports our Strategic Plan.
- Our workforce has creative ideas that can transform how we deliver services.
- Investing in innovation empowers employees and boosts morale.
- Cross-division collaboration strengthens problem-solving and reduces silos.

## Developing Your Proposal
- All ideas are welcome — bold concepts encouraged.
- Proposals that support one or more Strategic Focus Areas are encouraged: Resiliency, Sustainability, Customer Experience, Equity, Financial Health.
- Innovations may be internal, external, hybrid, community-focused, and/or staff-focused.
- Only one submission is needed per Innovation Team.

## Submission Form: Required Information
Use this as a checklist when completing the official form.

1. Team Name (required)
2. Team Captain (required): name, email, division
3. Team Members (required): each member’s name, email, division
4. Best Way to Contact the Team Captain (required): Email, Text, or Microsoft Teams
5. Team Captain’s Contact Information (required): provide details if Email or Text selected
6. Proposal Title (required)
7. Brief Summary (required, 150–250 words)
8. Issue or Opportunity Addressed (required, 100–150 words)
9. How the Innovation Addresses the Issue (required)
10. Strategic Focus Areas (required; select all that apply): Sustainability, Resilience, Equity, Financial Health, Customer Experience
11. Alignment Explanation (required, 150–250 words): how your proposal supports the selected Focus Areas
12. Initial Implementation Process (required, 150–250 words)
13. Anticipated Challenges (required): e.g., funding, staff capacity, technical expertise
14. Needed Resources (required): e.g., SMEs, prototyping materials, outreach support
15. Additional Attachments (optional): up to 5 files; max 10MB each; allowed types: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, PDF, Image, Video, Audio

## Deadline
Target submission: January 29.
