<h2 style="margin-top: var(--spacing-3xl);">Draft Content</h2>

<p>
  Below are draft responses based on our research. These will be refined before submission.
</p>

<div class="card">
  <div class="card-header">
    <span class="card-icon">üìù</span>
    <h3>Brief Summary (Draft)</h3>
  </div>
  <p>
    We propose piloting a federated social media presence for Public Works using the ActivityPub 
    protocol (e.g., Mastodon). This would create an additional, accessible channel for residents 
    to follow official updates, alerts, and engage with Public Works‚Äîwithout relying exclusively 
    on corporate social media platforms.
  </p>
  <p>
    Federated social media is decentralized, open-source, and ad-free, aligning with public 
    sector values of transparency and accessibility. The pilot would validate fit, document 
    requirements (hosting, moderation, accessibility, records retention), and recommend whether 
    to proceed with full implementation.
  </p>
  <p>
    This concept has been externally validated: it received an Innovation Award at the Pasadena 
    City College BET Club / SBDC business pitch contest. We're developing this proposal in the 
    open on GitHub to model transparency and invite collaboration.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <div class="card-header">
    <span class="card-icon">üéØ</span>
    <h3>Issue or Opportunity (Draft)</h3>
  </div>
  <p>
    Traditional social media platforms are algorithm-driven, increasingly fragmented, and some 
    residents have privacy or policy concerns that lead them to avoid these platforms entirely. 
    This creates a gap in reaching all community members.
  </p>
  <p>
    The fediverse offers an opportunity to reach these residents on a platform that aligns with 
    government values: open-source, transparent, and user-controlled. It also provides resilience 
    against platform policy changes or shutdowns.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <div class="card-header">
    <span class="card-icon">üß≠</span>
    <h3>Strategic Alignment (Draft)</h3>
  </div>
  <ul class="prose">
    <li><strong>Customer Experience:</strong> Meet residents where they are, including those who prefer open platforms</li>
    <li><strong>Sustainability:</strong> Build on open-source infrastructure that isn't dependent on any single company</li>
    <li><strong>Resilience:</strong> Diversify communication channels; not all eggs in one corporate basket</li>
  </ul>
</div>
